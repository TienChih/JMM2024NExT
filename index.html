Suppose that a car drives east between \(40\)mph and \(55\)mph for an hour, then between \(60\)mph and \(80\)mph for 2 hours.

Whats the least distance they could have traveled? Whats the farthest they could have traveled?

This doesn't seem super exact, if you pick a distance in this range and then are told the actual distance, what is the farthest off that you could be?

Suppose a particles velocity is \(v(t)=t^2\)m/s \(t\) seconds after we start measuring.

If \(t\) is in \([0,1]\) whats the lowest the particles velocity could be? The highest? What about in \([1,3]\)?

If \(t\) is in \([0,1]\) whats the shortest distance the particles velocity could have traveled? The farthest? What about in \([1,3]\)?

What are the errors in these cases?

If \(v\) is a constant, then the change of a particles position in \(\Delta t\) seconds moving at \(v\)m/s is \[\Delta x = v\Delta t.\]

If \(\underline{v}\leq v \leq \overline{v}\) then the change of a particles position in \(\Delta t\) seconds moving at \(v\)m/s satisfies \[\underline{v}\Delta t\leq v\Delta t \leq \overline{v} \Delta t.\]

Consider \(v(t)=t^2\) over \(t\) in \([0,2]\) Let \(n=4\). If we subdivide \([0,2]\) into 4 pieces, we get 4 regions \[[t_{i-1}, t_{i}]\] where \(t_0=0, t_1=0.5, t_2=1, t_3=1.5, t_4=2.\)

For each of the 4 regions region \([t_{i-1}, t_i]\) find a lower bound \(\underline{v}_i\) and upper bound \(\overline{v}_i\) for the velocity in that region.

For each of the 4 regions region \([t_{i-1}, t_i]\) find a lower bound and upper bound for the distance traveled in that region.

Find a lower and upper bound for the distance they could have traveled.

Bump \(n\) up a few at a time. What happens?
